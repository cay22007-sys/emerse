Learn as You Save

📊
Fast Fact
24% of American consumers have no emergency savings at all — EMERSE exists to change that statistic, one person at a time.
— Bankrate Emergency Fund Survey
👨‍👩‍👧‍👦
Fast Fact
16% of Baby Boomers, 24% of Gen Xers, 28% of Millennials, and 34% of Gen Zers have no emergency savings at all.
— Bankrate Generational Savings Report
💳
Fast Fact
1 in 3 Americans have more credit card debt than emergency savings — a dangerous position when income shocks strike.
— Federal Reserve Survey of Consumer Finances
📐
Fast Fact
The rule of thumb is to put away at least three to six months' worth of living expenses — adjusted for your personal risk profile.
— CFP Board Guidelines
🏦
Fast Fact
Account holders do not need to prove it is an emergency to make a withdrawal from their savings account — your money is always yours.
— FDIC Consumer Resources
🎯
Savings Suggestion
Define what an emergency means to you before a crisis hits — so you only use your funds on something truly crucial, not impulse spending.
— EMERSE Financial Coaches
🚫
Savings Suggestion
Avoid keeping emergency savings in cash, illiquid accounts, or risky investments. Accessibility and stability must be the priority.
— Consumer Financial Protection Bureau
🎁
Expert Advice
Take advantage of one-time opportunities to save — such as tax refunds, cash gifts, or reimbursements. These windfalls can dramatically accelerate your fund.
— IRS Taxpayer Guidance
⚡
Expert Advice
Spending shocks are unplanned expenses. Income shocks are the unplanned loss of income. Your emergency fund must protect against both types of disruption.
— Urban Institute Financial Research
💡
Pro Tip
Try "Pay Yourself First" — automatically transfer savings the moment your paycheck lands, before any other spending occurs. It changes everything.
— Personal Finance Best Practices
🏦
High-Yield Savings
Keep your fund in a High-Yield Savings Account earning 4–5% APY. Your money grows while staying fully accessible anytime.
📉
Recession Strategy
During recessions, inferior goods consumption rises. EMERSE helps you model spending shifts before shocks happen — not after.
🧬
Health Risk Planning
Family medical history significantly impacts emergency costs. We factor genetic and chronic condition risks directly into your personal plan.
